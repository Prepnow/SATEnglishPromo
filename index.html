The 680 Strategy

Why English comes first.

Most students jump straight into math. But the fastest path to a strong SAT composite starts with securing a 680 in Reading & Writing first.

  • 1English is learnable in a short window. The R&W section tests a finite system: 4 domains, 6 trap types, and ~650 high-frequency words. Every skill is teachable and measurable. Math requires years of prior knowledge — a longer arc that cannot be compressed into five days.
  • 2Sharper reading raises your math score too. Many SAT math errors are not calculation mistakes — they are misreadings of the question. Students who build reading precision in English see their math word problem accuracy rise as a direct result.
  • 3A solid English floor makes your composite resilient. When your R&W baseline is 680 or above, you approach math with confidence. Balanced composite scores also read more consistently to admissions officers than one strong section propping up a weak one.
  • 4Spring Break is the right time to build this foundation. AP season begins in May. Once it does, bandwidth for SAT prep collapses. Five focused days on English now creates the score base you build math on top of — in the order that works.
AP

The Window Before AP Season

AP exams typically begin in early May — and once that season starts, meaningful SAT preparation becomes nearly impossible. Spring Break is the last clear, uninterrupted stretch before AP prep consumes everything. Students who use it to lock in their English score can approach their next SAT date with one section already settled, freeing all their post-AP energy for math. This is the sequence that actually produces composite score gains.
