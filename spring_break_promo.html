The 680 Strategy

Why English comes first.

Most students jump straight into math. But the fastest path to a strong SAT composite starts with securing a 680 in Reading & Writing first — and there are clear, measurable reasons why.

  • 1 English is learnable in a short window. The R&W section tests a finite system: 4 domains, 6 trap types, and roughly 650 high-frequency words. Every skill is teachable and measurable. Math, by contrast, requires building on years of prior knowledge — a longer arc that can't be compressed into five days.
  • 2 Sharper reading raises your math score too. Many SAT math errors aren't calculation mistakes — they're misreadings of the question. Students who build strong reading precision in English find their math word problem accuracy rises as a direct result. English prep is never wasted.
  • 3 A solid English floor makes your total score resilient. When you know your R&W baseline is 680 or above, you approach math with confidence rather than anxiety. Balanced composite scores also read more consistently to admissions officers than one section propping up a weak one.
  • 4 Spring Break is the right time to build this foundation. AP season begins in May. Once it does, bandwidth for SAT prep collapses. Five focused days on English now creates the score base you build math on top of — in the order that actually works.

The Window Before AP Season

AP exams typically begin in early May — and once that season starts, meaningful SAT preparation becomes nearly impossible. Spring Break is the last clear, uninterrupted stretch before AP prep consumes everything. Students who use it to lock in their English score can approach their next SAT date with one section already settled, freeing all their post-AP energy for math. This is the sequence that actually produces composite score gains — and spring break is exactly when it should happen.
